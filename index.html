About Me

I'm a third-year Computer Engineering student currently on leave until Spring 2026 to gain practical experience and invest in personal development. Dedicated to self-reflection, self-discovery, and self-improvement, I enjoy applying my skills in software engineering, AI, cybersecurity, and productivity to solve real-world problems and help people learn, grow, and stay secure.

As a Security Analyst Intern at Jackson’s Security Operations Center, I’ve investigated 650+ phishing emails using threat intelligence tools (e.g., malware scanners, header analyzers, IP data providers) to protect company stakeholders from social engineering attacks. I’ve identified and escalated business email compromises that helped safeguard over $500k in client assets and was entrusted with training two new interns, combining my technical experience and teaching ability.

As an AI Trainer, I evaluated and fine-tuned 10+ large language models (text-to-text and text-to-image) using prompt engineering strategies like few-shot learning and chain-of-thought prompting. I optimized model outputs using reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) and conducted peer QA reviews to ensure safety, consistency, and model alignment.

As a Controls Engineer Intern at Lear Corporation, I discovered and resolved a code bug in PLC ladder logic, prompting a company-wide investigation. I prepared technical reports and presentations for engineers and upper management, analyzed camera data to improve seat assembly processes, and developed a PFMEA contingency plan to ensure continuity in seat-shipping across 30+ trailers.

As President of MSU’s NSBE Chapter, I secured 97% support for my presidency and assembled the first fully staffed board since 2019. I implemented a task-management system and streamlined communication processes, supporting initiatives that led to multiple awards, including MSU Outstanding Academic Organization of the Year, MSU Outstanding Professional Organization of the Year, and 2nd Place for NSBE Region IV Chapter of the Year.

As a STEM Tutor for TRIO SSS at MSU, I led 30+ tutoring sessions in calculus and programming. I taught study strategies like active recall, spaced repetition, and the Feynman Technique while introducing tools like Notion and the Pomodoro Method to support time management and independent learning.

At the KPMG Future of Tech Summit Case Competition in Orlando, FL, I collaborated with a cross-disciplinary team to develop a solution for community carbon neutrality, delivering a compelling presentation that led us to place as finalists.
