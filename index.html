About Me

I am a second-year Computer Engineering student at Michigan State University utilizing my values of self-reflection, self-discovery, and self-improvement to develop professionally and acquire the skills needed to excel in engineering.

Those values have led me to develop strong discipline, work ethic, problem-solving, and communication. Additionally, they have cultivated within me a passion for acquiring a deep understanding and becoming the best version of myself.

To achieve this, I participate in seminars and leadership programs. I graduated from the Grand Rapids Initiative for Leaders and the Office of Cultural & Academic Transitions Leadership Institute, where I learned about leadership and learning styles, inclusive and cross-cultural leadership, in conjunction with interpersonal skills. I attended KPMG's Future of Tech Summit, where I expanded my network and received valuable insight into communication, management, and research. Additionally, I studied in Paris, France, to expose myself to new experiences and increase my cross-cultural competency.

I am also passionate about helping others on their journeys of self-reflection, self-discovery, and self-improvement, which stems from my values of camaraderie and authenticity.

Consequently, I serve in several capacities that involve connecting with and helping others. I am a tutor for TRIO Student Support Services, a program that provides resources for first-generation, low-income, and disabled students. I also serve as Senator and BSU Liaison for the Michigan State University chapter of the National Society of Engineers. I sit on the chapter's Academic Excellence Committee. By serving in these capacities, I aim to use and spread the knowledge and techniques I've learned regarding academic excellence, personal growth, and advocacy to help others achieve success.

In the future, I plan to continue participating in professional and leadership programs. I will hone my skills with Python, Verilog, and digital logic. I aim to learn C and develop my knowledge of hardware using FPGAs. I intend to obtain an internship for Summer 2023 related to computer engineering.

Please don't hesitate to reach out if you would like to talk.
